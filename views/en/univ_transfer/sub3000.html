Adult Study Programs

English Studies Program (ESL)

“The first step toward Canadian university success — start with strong English skills!”

The English Studies Program is an intensive ESL (English as a Second Language) course for international students who want to improve English proficiency for college, university, or everyday communication in English-speaking environments.
The curriculum progresses from Beginner to University Preparation, helping students develop balanced skills in reading, writing, listening, and speaking.

Program Objectives

  • Prepare for success in Canadian college/university-level academic English
  • Strengthen practical skills in essay writing, presentations, discussions, and research
  • Build communication skills essential for school, work, and daily life in English-speaking countries
Many students complete this program before advancing to the High School Program or University Transfer / AA Program.
Program Structure

The program consists of four levels — Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, and University Preparation — determined by a placement test at admission.

Beginner
  • Focus on basic grammar, vocabulary, pronunciation, and sentence construction.
  • Students gain confidence in everyday conversation.
Intermediate
  • Integrated training across reading, writing, listening, and speaking.
  • Students improve written and spoken expression.
  • At this stage, students may choose two electives (e.g., Business, Media, Culture) to learn practical English through real-life topics.
Advanced
  • Emphasis on academic writing, critical reading, presentations, and research projects.
  • Prepares students for the University Preparation stage.
University Preparation (ENGL 099)
  • Students take ENGL 099 (college-prep English) along with 2–3 university-transferable courses.
  • Completion of this level qualifies students for direct admission into college/university programs.
Program Features
  • Small Class Size

Personalized attention and active teacher–student interaction

  • Individualized Learning

Curriculum tailored to each student’s goals and strengths

  • Multicultural Environment

Learn alongside peers from around the world

  • Seamless Pathway

Smooth transition to college programs (High School or University Transfer)

  • Flexible Duration

Options for 1-month intensive or 4-month semester-based study

  • Convenient Location & Support

Downtown Vancouver campus, homestay options, and settlement orientation

  • Certificate of Completion

Issued upon successful completion of each level

Admission Requirements
  • Minimum age: 15
  • Take the College English Diagnostic Test for placement
  • No external English score required (placement by internal testing)
  • Full-time study: at least 20 hours per week
Recommended For
  • Students preparing for college/university study in Canada who want structured English training
  • Students with limited overseas experience needing language and cultural adjustment
  • Adult learners seeking short-term intensive English improvement for study or career goals
