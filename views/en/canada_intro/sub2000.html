Vancouver: Your Top Study Destination

Vancouver is Canada’s third-largest city after Toronto and Montreal, yet it is consistently ranked as the most livable city in Canada thanks to its mild climate, stunning natural scenery, and high quality of life.
Each year, Vancouver is also ranked among the Top 3 “Most Livable Cities in the World”, making it an attractive destination for international students and immigrants alike.

British Columbia and Metro Vancouver

British Columbia (BC), where Vancouver is located, can be divided into Vancouver city,
Metro Vancouver (Greater Vancouver Area), and surrounding suburban regions.
Metro Vancouver includes Burnaby, Richmond, North Vancouver, West Vancouver, Coquitlam, Surrey, Langley, and Delta.
Each local school district operates independent admission systems for international students.

Vancouver School Board (VSB)
  • Recognized as one of Canada’s most prestigious school districts.
  • Located near UBC (University of British Columbia), offering an exceptional academic environment.
  • Close to downtown, with easy access to culture, arts, and leisure activities.
  • Surrounded by beaches and large parks, providing an excellent lifestyle setting.
  • Although tuition and housing costs are relatively high, it remains one of the most popular districts among international families.
  • School Grade System: Elementary (Gr.1–7), Secondary (Gr.8–12)
North Vancouver
School District
  • Renowned for its beautiful natural environment.
  • Within 30 minutes of ski resorts (Grouse Mountain, Cypress, Mount Seymour), making it ideal for winter sports.
  • Close to downtown, with excellent libraries and community facilities.
  • Known for strong academic performance and a safe, family-friendly atmosphere, highly popular among Korean parents.
  • School Grade System: Elementary (Gr.1–7), Secondary (Gr.8–12)
Coquitlam School District
  • Covers three cities: Coquitlam, Port Moody, and Port Coquitlam.
  • Home to a large Korean community, with many amenities such as Korean supermarkets, academies, and restaurants.
  • Academic system divided into Elementary (Gr.1–5), Middle (Gr.6–8), Secondary (Gr.9–12).
  • Strong programs in arts, music, and sports, ideal for students seeking a well-rounded education.
Burnaby School District
  • Highly regarded for strong IB (International Baccalaureate) and AP (Advanced Placement) programs.
  • Large population of students from China and other Asian countries, creating a diverse, global learning atmosphere.
  • Conveniently located between Vancouver and Coquitlam, with excellent transportation and living infrastructure.
  • Residential environment balanced with high-rise condos, large shopping malls, supermarkets, parks, and nearby mountains.
Surrey School District
  • The largest school district in BC, offering a wide range of international student programs.
  • Significant South Asian(Indian) community, with increasing numbers of Korean and Canadian families.
  • South Surrey/White Rock: Pleasant climate, close to the U.S. border, and high living convenience.
  • North Surrey: Adjacent to Coquitlam, offering good accessibility.
  • Relatively affordable tuition with diverse school options.
Langley School District
  • Located about one hour from downtown Vancouver, known for its safe and comfortable living environment.
  • Rapidly developing residential areas with improving education and community infrastructure.
  • Relatively few Korean students, making it favorable for full English immersion.
  • School Grade System: Elementary (Gr.1–5), Middle (Gr.6–8), Secondary (Gr.9–12).
Maple Ridge–Pitt Meadows School District
  • Suburban setting with comparatively lower housing costs.
  • Offers abundant sports and outdoor activities such as horseback riding, hiking, and water sports, allowing for a balanced lifestyle.
  • Popular among families choosing parent-accompanied study abroad and supervised guardianship programs.
Abbotsford School District
  • Located about 1 hour 20 minutes east of Metro Vancouver, part of the Fraser Valley Regional District.
  • Smaller Asian population creates an ideal environment for English immersion.
  • Affordable living and rental costs with a safe, small-city atmosphere.
  • Well-suited for supervised guardianship and homestay programs.
