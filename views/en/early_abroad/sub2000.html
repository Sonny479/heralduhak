Tuition-Free Public Education for Children

In Canada, children are eligible for free public elementary, middle, and high school education
if their parents are: Enrolled in a government-approved degree program (College/University:
Diploma, Bachelor, Master, PhD) or Residing in Canada with a valid work permit (LMIA-based or Open Work Permit).

This system allows children to gain valuable North American public school experience while significantly reducing tuition costs.

※ If parents are enrolled only in ESL or language training programs, children must pay international student tuition (approx. CAD $15,000–20,000 per year).

Academic System & Admission Timeline
  • School Year Start: First week of September (regular academic calendar)
  • Application Deadlines: 
    For September intake: applications open August 1 of the previous year
    For January/February intake: applications open April 15 of the previous year and close October 31
  • School Placement: Determined by catchment area, based on residential address
Parent Benefits
  • Legal Stay + Work Opportunities
    During the academic term: up to 24 hours per week
    During school breaks: up to 40 hours per week
  • Pathway to Permanent Residency (PR): Academic and work experience may strengthen eligibility for Express Entry or Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) applications.
